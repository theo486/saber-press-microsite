Long (≈90 words)

Saber (Sabermine Ltd) is the workflow engine for high-volume claims and collections. Built for operations handling millions of near-identical cases on a deadline, it automates the document- and process-heavy work — intake, triage, eligibility, evidence and payout — while keeping a human in control of every judgement call, and sits on top of the systems firms already run rather than replacing them. Founded in Manchester in 2024, Saber reached £26m in revenue in two years and was named 2026 North West Technology StartUp of the Year and Overall StartUp of the Year. It is now extending its proven claims engine into debt collection and insurance.

Product

What Saber does.

Saber reads inbound correspondence and documents, extracts and classifies them, runs the routine steps (eligibility, evidence, next action), and surfaces the exceptions and judgement calls to a person to approve — with 100% QA and a time-stamped audit trail behind every action. It deploys in weeks, sits above existing case-management and core systems, and scales to millions of cases without scaling headcount.

The story / news hook

Why Saber, why now.

A Manchester AI startup, founded in 2024, has reached £26m in revenue in two years by solving an unglamorous but enormous problem: the gap between a document arriving and a person working it, across millions of claims. In 2026 it was named the North West's Technology StartUp of the Year and its Overall StartUp of the Year — and is now taking its proven claims engine into two markets under acute pressure: debt collection and insurance.
